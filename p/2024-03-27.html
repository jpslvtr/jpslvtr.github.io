An open letter to the men who date, but not for marriage

Mar 27, 2024

Lately, I've been listening to several female friends discuss their relationship problems. Turns out, I have some thoughts on this topic. This post isn't for those of you on a “mission”, for whom a relationship feels like a distraction. I get it, and I respect that. This is for the men who spend their time dating women with no intention of marrying them. So if you are these men, (or just here for the ride), stick around.

Transitioning from school to work is like entering an unspoken contract where you’re expected to be responsible to a certain degree. As you age, the expectations will grow, nudging you towards contributing to society. While some may opt out of this path, most follow the crowd. Similarly, just as you graduate from school to start a career, it’s natural to evolve from casual dating to seeking a serious, marriage-oriented relationship. I don’t have much evidence to prove this point; only the lived experiences of those around me and a belief that, at some point, this shift makes sense.

“What if I pick the wrong one?”

Choosing to date with marriage in mind is precisely about avoiding the mistake of picking the wrong partner. A practical step is to seek the wisdom from happily married men in your circle, asking them what qualities to look for. Your parents can also be valuable resources; they have more life experience, and naturally, they want the best for you. Finding 'the one' isn’t that hard, especially if you have your shit together. I subscribe to a somewhat traditional view that in a relationship, a man should lead. By leading, I mean navigating life together, hand in hand, with you as a guide. A man who is self-aware will find it much easier to support whoever his partner is, paving the way for a more fulfilling marriage.

“Why not just enjoy a long-term relationship without the label of marriage?”

Consider this: you’re in a long-term relationship with a woman, and you have children together. If you're hesitant to marry, it’s probably because you want an easy way out, should things go south - avoiding legal and financial hassles. Is this the kind of commitment you want to offer to someone you love? Marriage symbolizes dedication. Think from a child’s perspective: wouldn’t you prefer to see your parents fully committed to each other?

“Why be monogamous at all?”

I defer to insights from those far wiser than myself.

Growing up, marriage was never a priority. I rarely saw examples of good marriages and felt that pursuing my ambitions was incompatible with romantic relationships. However, as I got older, watching my morally right and successful friends getting married made me question my earlier beliefs about marriage. I also made the realization that I probably want children. Isn’t being a father the most (only) meaningful pursuit we can undertake in life?

This realization flipped a switch in me, steering me towards dating for marriage. Since making that shift, I've found greater happiness, confirming it was one of the best decisions I've ever made.

As an introvert, I valued my bachelor life. Not for the freedom to indulge in pleasures, but for the peace solitude brought me. I thought that marriage would be challenging. And now that I am married, I admit there was an adjustment period to sharing my life so closely with another person. However, this new chapter has become a new norm that only enriches my life. A good spouse is an invaluable gift; being married means having the ultimate supporter, a devoted partner who not only accepts you as you are but also inspires you to grow. Facing life’s challenges becomes a shared journey rather than a solitary battle. That being said, choosing the wrong partner can potentially be very bad, underscoring the importance of making the right choice.

I’ve only been married for like ten minutes. I could be mistaken about some or all of what I’ve said. However, so far, it has held true for me and my friends.
